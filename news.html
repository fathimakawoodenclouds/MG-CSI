National Law Fest 2024

The National Law Fest 2024 was a grand success, bringing together students and legal luminaries from across the country. The fest featured a variety of competitions including moot courts, client counseling, and debates, alongside vibrant cultural performances that showcased the diverse talents of the participants.

The fest attracted participation from over 50 law colleges across the nation. Dignitaries from the High Court graced the inaugural ceremony, emphasizing the importance of practical legal training. The event provided a platform for students to network and learning from their peers.

The valedictory session saw the distribution of awards to the winners. The rolling trophy was bagged by the team from National Law School. The organizers were lauded for their meticulous planning and execution of the mega event.

Wildlife Protection Seminar

The ECO Club organized an insightful seminar focused on wildlife protection and conservation. Experts shared their knowledge on the current struggles in wildlife preservation and discussed actionable steps for students to contribute to a sustainable future.

The seminar highlighted the threats posed by poaching and habitat loss. Speakers urged students to become advocates for wildlife conservation. Documentaries showcasing the effort of forest rangers were screened to inspire the audience.

An interactive session allowed students to propose their own ideas for campus-based conservation initiatives. The ECO Club announced a series of follow-up activities, including a nature walk and a photography contest, to keep the momentum going.

Blood Donation Awareness

A legal aid camp was organized in the nearby village to provide free legal assistance to the underprivileged. Students and faculty members actively participated in creating awareness about basic legal rights and remedies.

The camp aimed to bridge the gap between the legal system and the common people. Volunteers provided guidance on various legal issues, including property disputes, family matters, and consumer rights. The initiative was well-received by the community, highlighting the institute's commitment to social responsibility.

Local authorities and legal practitioners also supported the event, offering pro bono consultations. The students gained valuable practical experience, understanding the real-world challenges faced by marginalized communities in accessing justice.

World Environment Day Celebration

The inauguration of the new academic block marks a significant milestone in the infrastructure development of MGCSI Institute. The state-of-the-art facility includes modern classrooms, a digital library, and a moot court hall.

The new block is designed to provide a conducive learning environment for students. It is equipped with the latest technology to support modern teaching methodologies. The digital library offers access to a vast collection of legal resources, while the moot court hall provides a realistic setting for students to practice their advocacy skills.

Furthermore, the facility houses dedicated research centers and seminar halls to encourage academic discourse. The administration emphasized that this expansion aligns with the college's vision of becoming a center of excellence in legal education.

Moot Court Society Orientation

An orientation session was organized for the new members of the Moot Court Society. Senior members and faculty guides introduced the students to the nuances of mooting, legal research, and courtroom etiquette.

The session covered the basics of drafting memorials and presenting oral arguments. New members were given a roadmap of upcoming moot court competitions and training workshops. The orientation aimed to inspire and equip students to excel in mooting activities.

The session also included a mock moot court demonstration, allowing new members to observe experienced students in action. This practical exposure was highly beneficial in demystifying the mooting process and encouraging active participation.
