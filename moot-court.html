Practical Advocacy Training

Moot Court

Realistic Courtroom Experience

Moot Court gives students a realistic courtroom experience, helping them practice legal arguments, case analysis, and presentation skills in a structured environment.

Our moot court program is designed to bridge the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application, providing students with hands-on experience in legal advocacy and courtroom procedures.

Developing confident advocates through practical training and competitive exposure.
What We Offer

Key Features / Highlights

Practical Advocacy

Students present cases before mock judges, developing confidence and clarity in legal argumentation and courtroom presentation.

Legal Research

Encourages in-depth study of laws, precedents, and case strategies, enhancing research skills and legal knowledge.

Oral & Written Skills

Improves drafting of pleadings, memos, and oral submissions, developing comprehensive legal communication abilities.

Competitions

Participation in internal and inter-college moot contests to gain exposure, experience, and recognition in competitive advocacy.

Mentorship

Guidance from faculty and legal experts to refine skills, improve performance, and develop professional advocacy techniques.

Team Collaboration

Working in teams fosters collaboration, strategic thinking, and the ability to work effectively with peers on complex legal issues.

Benefits of Moot Court

  • Builds confidence in public speaking and legal argumentation
  • Enhances critical thinking and analytical skills
  • Provides practical courtroom experience before graduation
  • Develops professional networking opportunities
  • Improves time management and teamwork abilities
  • Strengthens resume and career prospects
